Change what you think you know!
לֹֽא־ תְבַשֵּׁ֥ל גְּדִ֖י בַּחֲלֵ֥ב אִמֹּֽו
You shall not boil a kid in its mother's milk

This line appears in the Torah three times, and is the source from which the Rabbis derive the prohibition on eating milk and meat together.

But chickens do not produce milk, so why are we told that we cannot consume them together?

It was not always this way.

Jews of the ancient world debated the meaning of this passage of the Torah. Philo of Alexandria posited that the prohibition was to be taken literally, as not to boil an animal in its own mother's milk.

The Rabbis continue to debate this issue throughout the talmudic period, positing different opinions on the permissibility of chicken and milk together. Under some Rabbis it was permitted, under others, it was not.

But then things began to change.

One Rabbi opined in the Talmud:

עוף וגבינה נאכלין באפיקורן
Chicken and cheese can be eaten together with abandon!

But the Talmud hastily adds that this means that they cannot be eaten together, and “with abandon” simply means that one does not have to wash their hands or rinse their mouth in between the consumption of chicken and dairy.
